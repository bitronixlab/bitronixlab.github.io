Founder

Built by Stijo Joseph for makers who want ideas to become real systems.

Bitronix Lab is shaped around practical engineering: elegant circuits, teachable concepts, working prototypes, and a brand voice that feels futuristic without losing clarity.

Stijo Joseph is an embedded systems engineer and educator who builds microcontroller-based products, sensor networks, and AI-enabled prototypes end to end. His work spans hardware design — schematic capture, PCB layout, bring-up, and validation — and firmware on Arduino, STM32, and ESP32 platforms. Stijo focuses on bringing applied AI to constrained edge devices, integrating computer vision, signal processing, and on-device inference into real-world systems. Alongside engineering, he teaches practical electronics, RTOS workflows, and embedded AI through workshops and structured tracks at Bitronix Lab. He believes the best way to learn hardware is to build, measure, and ship — and his projects reflect that. Through Bitronix Lab he helps founders, students, and teams turn ideas into reliable, instrumented systems they can confidently explain and improve.
